FAQ

Frequently asked
questions.

What makes a product AI-native vs. just AI-enabled?

+

AI-enabled products bolt a model onto an existing feature — a chatbot in the corner, a "summarize" button. AI-native products are designed around model behavior from day one: the data model anticipates training signals, the UX teaches the model through interaction, and the business model accommodates per-request compute cost. The difference shows up in retention and unit economics two years in.

How do you handle multi-tenant AI — shared models or per-customer?

+

Depends on the feature. We use shared foundation models with per-tenant prompts and retrieval, tenant-scoped fine-tuning when accuracy demands it, and fully isolated deployments for regulated industries. We design the tenancy model up front so you don't have to re-architect when your biggest customer asks for a dedicated instance.

What's the right build-vs-buy call for AI features?

+

Buy the commodity, build the differentiator. Foundation models, vector databases, observability — buy them. The prompt strategies, retrieval logic, evaluation harnesses, and domain-specific fine-tunes that make your product uniquely good — build those. We help you draw that line based on your moat, not the vendor pitch you heard last week.

How do we price AI features that cost us money per call?

+

Meter actual usage, not seats. We instrument cost-per-request from day one and build pricing that maps to consumption — credits, tiered limits, or usage-based addons. We also build the cost-control layer: caching, cheaper fallback models, and circuit breakers so a single enterprise user can't burn through your margin.

How do we prevent AI features from shipping and then rotting?

+

We build the ops layer alongside the feature. Quality evaluations, drift detection, user feedback loops, cost monitoring, and model version pinning so you know when something changes. AI features degrade silently — we make degradation visible so your team can catch it before your users do.
