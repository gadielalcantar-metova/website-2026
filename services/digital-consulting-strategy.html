FAQ

Frequently asked
questions.

What does a digital consulting engagement look like?

+

It depends on where you are. Some engagements start with a technology audit — we review your stack, architecture, and team structure and deliver a clear picture of what's working and what's creating risk. Others start with product strategy: defining what to build next, in what order, and why. We scope each engagement to the specific decision you're trying to make.

How is this different from a traditional consulting firm?

+

We build what we recommend. Every piece of advice we give is grounded in 18 years of actually shipping software — not frameworks and slide decks. We've made the architecture mistakes, the hiring mistakes, the scope mistakes. We help you avoid them, and if you want us to execute on the strategy, we can do that too.

Do you work with early-stage startups or established companies?

+

Both. Early-stage companies come to us to define product direction, make their first technology hires, and avoid expensive early architecture mistakes. Established companies come to us when they're scaling, modernizing legacy systems, or evaluating a major technology decision — like whether to build, buy, or partner.

Can you act as a fractional CTO?

+

Yes. We've served as embedded technology leadership for companies that need senior engineering judgment without a full-time hire. This includes team structure decisions, vendor evaluation, board-level technology presentations, and architecture oversight — as much or as little as the situation requires.

What deliverables come out of a consulting engagement?

+

It depends on the engagement, but common outputs include: technology audits with risk ratings, product roadmaps with prioritization rationale, architecture diagrams and decision records, build-vs-buy analyses, and hiring plans with role definitions. Everything is documented and actionable — not abstract.
