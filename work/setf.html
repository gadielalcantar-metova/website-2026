The Syrian Emergency Task Force needed more than strategy — they needed technology that could protect lives. Metova's volunteer team built Syria Watch: a real-time mobile alert system for civilians in conflict zones.

Founded in March 2011, the Syrian Emergency Task Force is a civil organization dedicated to supporting the Syrian people's demand for freedom and dignity. Their humanitarian work centers on the most vulnerable — internally displaced people, orphans, besieged civilians, and those in greatest need. In an active conflict environment, accurate and timely information about armed attacks isn't just useful. It's lifesaving.

SETF approached Metova for help building a mobile application that could deliver real-time conflict alerts to civilians. Metova answered through MetoValue — the company's volunteer program that dedicates engineering talent to causes that matter. The team provided strategic guidance, design expertise, and full-cycle native development to bring Syria Watch to life on both iOS and Android.

The Challenge

In an active conflict zone, slow information is no information at all.

The ongoing conflict in Syria created a critical gap between the people on the ground and the organizations trying to protect them. Civilians in affected areas had no reliable, real-time channel to receive accurate information about active or incoming armed attacks. That gap cost lives.

SETF had the organizational infrastructure and the mission — but they needed the technical capability to turn that mission into a functional mobile product. The app had to be fast, secure, and simple enough to work under crisis conditions. It also had to be built with almost no commercial budget, relying entirely on Metova's volunteer commitment to deliver something genuinely production-ready.

The Solution

Syria Watch — native iOS and Android built to protect, inform, and empower.

Metova's MetoValue team approached Syria Watch the same way they approach every commercial engagement: with the full weight of their product design and engineering process. The team conducted research and strategic assessment before writing a single line of code, ensuring the app was built around the real needs of the people it served.

Syria Watch delivers real-time push notifications about active and potential armed attacks — mapped to specific locations with detailed context about attack type and activity history. The secure application architecture was designed to maintain reliability under the conditions its users would actually face. The design was kept deliberately simple: every second of cognitive load removed was a second closer to safety.

Both iOS and Android versions were built natively, ensuring maximum performance and reliability on the devices most commonly used in the region. The result is a tool that puts critical, accurate, location-specific information in the hands of the people who need it most — exactly when they need it.
